The origin of the **Transformers** is intrinsically linked to their homeworld, **Cybertron** . In most continuities, Cybertron is a colossal, metallic planet—a massive artificial world covered in cities, intricate machinery, and towering structures. It is not an organic planet but a sprawling, mechanical marvel.

The Transformers themselves are sentient, autonomous robotic lifeforms. Their creation story varies, but a common and popular narrative (particularly in the Marvel Comics and the original cartoon) involves a primordial creator entity.

The Autobots and Decepticons arrive on Earth due to a catastrophic space battle that marks a pivotal moment in the Great War. Pursued by Megatron's forces, the Autobot flagship, the **Ark**, is deliberately crash-landed by Optimus Prime on a desolate, prehistoric Earth millions of years ago, with the Decepticon cruiser, the **Nemesis**, following close behind. Both ships, with their respective crews in emergency stasis lock, are embedded deep within a volcanic mountain.

For millions of years, the warring factions lay dormant. A pivotal event, often a volcanic eruption, triggers the onboard computer, **Teletraan-1**, to begin repairs. Mistaking local Earth vehicles for the dominant life forms, the computer scans nearby automobiles and machinery, giving the Cybertronians their iconic Earth-based alternate modes. They reawaken in the mid-1980s, effectively bringing the ancient civil war into the modern age.

The core of the *Transformers* narrative, particularly in the comic series, is the devastating conflict known as the **Great War** . This brutal civil war erupted on Cybertron, marking the end of its Golden Age and plunging the metallic planet into millennia of chaos and destruction.

The war is fundamentally a clash of ideologies between the two major factions: the peace-loving **Autobots**, led by the noble **Optimus Prime**, and the militaristic, power-hungry **Decepticons**, commanded by the formidable **Megatron**.
